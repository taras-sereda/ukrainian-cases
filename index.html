How Cases Work

In English, word order tells you who does what: "The girl sees the boy" vs. "The boy sees the girl." In Ukrainian, word endings do that job instead. These ending changes are called cases (відмінки). Ukrainian has 7 of them. Each case answers a different question and serves a different role in the sentence.

When you look up a word in a dictionary, you find its Nominative form — that's the base form. All other cases modify the ending of that base form.

Each Case Explained

Nominative Називний

The base form — the dictionary form of the word. Used for the subject of a sentence (who or what performs the action).

Сестра читає книгу.
Sister reads a book.

Genitive Родовий

Shows possession ("of something"), absence ("there is no…"), origin ("from…"), or quantity. Think of English 's or "of."

Конституція України.
The Constitution of Ukraine.
Немає води.
There is no water.

Common prepositions: без (without), від (from), до (to/until), з (from/out of), після (after)

Dative Давальний

The indirect object — the person or thing receiving something. Think "to whom?" or "for whom?" The Ukrainian name comes from давати (to give).

Я дав книгу сестрі.
I gave a book to my sister.
Мамі подобається музика.
Mom likes music. (lit: Music is pleasing to Mom.)

Accusative Знахідний

The direct object — the thing being acted upon. Used with most action verbs.

Я люблю Україну.
I love Ukraine.
Хлопець читає книгу.
The boy reads a book.

Common prepositions: в/у (into), на (onto), за (for), через (through/because of)

Instrumental Орудний

Shows the tool or means by which something is done ("with what?"). Also used to say what you do professionally, or what's located above/below something.

Я пишу ручкою.
I write with a pen.
Вона працює вчителькою.
She works as a teacher.

Common prepositions: з (with), за (behind), між (between), над (above), під (under)

Locative Місцевий

Indicates location — where something is or happens. This case always requires a preposition (that's why it's sometimes called "Prepositional" in other Slavic languages).

Сумка в машині.
The bag is in the car.
Ми живемо в місті.
We live in a city.

Always with a preposition: в/у (in), на (on/at), по (along/after), при (by/near)

Vocative Кличний

Used when directly addressing someone or something. English doesn't change words for this — Ukrainian does.

Мамо, де ти?
Mom, where are you?
Слава Україні! — Героям слава!
Glory to Ukraine! — Glory to the heroes!

What Are Declensions?

Declensions are groups of nouns that follow the same pattern of ending changes. If you know what group a noun belongs to, you can predict all its case forms. Ukrainian has 4 groups — most are determined by the noun's gender and its ending in Nominative.

Don't try to memorize all the tables at once. Start by practicing common nouns in the flashcard modes, and the patterns will start to feel natural.
